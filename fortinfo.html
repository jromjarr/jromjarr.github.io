Fortnite is a video game which started off as a survival third person survival video game, which later took the world by storm with its free-to-play battle royale mode, which allowed players to fight for victory with 100 other players. It gained over 45 million players in its first 6 months, and became the most streamed game on livestreaming platform Twitch.

A screenshot of the in-game lobby in 2018. Cover art of Save the World, in 2017.

Due to the game's increasing popularity, it crossed over with popular pop culture franchises, the first of which was Avengers: Infinity War, which both helped to increase the game's popularity and to promote the upcoming movie. After the event's popularity, it got even more franchises to cross over with the popular video game, such as Star Wars and Marvel, along with DC, the latter of which have made comic book collaborations. It has also collaborated with popular anime franchises, such as Dragon Ball and Naruto.


On December 6, 2018, Creative mode as added, which allowed players to experiment and create their own maps and games within Fortnite, and this marked the first step towards the metaverse. Starting in December 2023, they announced a shift in focus in their game strategy, since they would be adding 3 brand new modes that would involve the metaverse as a focus. The new modes would be LEGO Fortnite, Fortnite Festival, and Rocket Racing. Each mode would bring in new gameplay styles, such as an exploration survival game, a rhythm game featuring popular artists, and a racing mode respectively. Their approach is to make Fortnite not just a battle royale game, but as a platform in which many games can be enjoyed, all in the same application, while also allowing creators to make and publish their own levels.

LEGO Fortnite, an exploration survival game. Fortnite Festival, a music rhythm game featuring artists like Billie Eilish. Rocket Racing, a game made by the creators of Rocket League.
